## 📄 SSPA Project Abstract for Call for Code 2026
Project Title: SSPA Human Rights Ledger
Primary UN SDG Focus: SDG 16 (Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions) [3.2]
Solution Tagline: Protect Identity. Preserve Evidence. Enable Justice. [3.2]
## 1. Challenge & Problem Statement
Every year, millions of vulnerable individuals lose access to their verifiable legal status, critical documentation, and protections due to sudden armed conflicts, climate-induced natural disasters, and sophisticated human trafficking networks. Current state-sponsored digital identity frameworks suffer from a fatal flaw: they are profoundly centralized and tied to vulnerable localized databases or single physical devices [3.2]. When a refugee's phone is confiscated at a volatile border, or local municipal servers are physically destroyed, survivors are instantly rendered "digitally invisible." This catastrophic loss of identity and evidentiary data creates an unyielding barrier to accessing international humanitarian aid pipelines, blocks effective post-disaster recovery, and permanently erases the chain of custody necessary for long-term global judicial accountability [3.2].
## 2. Technical Solution & Architecture
The SSPA Human Rights Ledger introduces a highly resilient, survivor-centered Distributed Trust Infrastructure purpose-built to operate under extreme, low-bandwidth, and completely disconnected environment scenarios (0 Kbps network partition) [3.2].
Instead of storing raw, vulnerable Personally Identifiable Information (PII) on an immutable public ledger, SSPA introduces the Survivor Identity Vault (SIV) [3.2]. The SIV encrypts sensitive demographic payloads on the client-side using X25519 asymmetric cryptography and signs field entries via Ed25519 curves [3.2].
To survive severe internet blackouts, local edge devices queue localized append-only incident logs [3.2]. These logs are systematically synchronized through an opportunistic, ad-hoc mesh network (Wi-Fi Direct/Bluetooth 5.0) utilizing a hybrid ordering protocol of Lamport timestamps and vector clocks to strictly prevent unauthorized history rewriting or local rollback attacks [3.2]. Once transient satellite or web connectivity is achieved by a Local Relay Node, batched transaction data is pushed as a single Merkle Root Anchor onto an EVM-compatible L2 settlement layer (chainId: 8080), ensuring fractional gas costs and absolute cryptographic integrity [3.2].
## 3. Real-World Impact & Long-Term Sustainability
SSPA transforms technology from a tool of surveillance into an unyielding shield for human dignity [3.2]. In real-world crises, if a field agent or survivor faces physical duress, entering a localized duress credential triggers an instant Cryptographic Shredding Protocol, turning localized vaults into unreadable random noise blobs while keeping the broader public ledger proofs intact [3.2]. If a device is lost, identity recovery is achieved through an automated Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) abstraction layer and multi-jurisdictional threshold signatures from trusted NGO validators, removing any dependency on compromised state agencies [3.2].
To ensure the infrastructure outlives the competition timeline and stands autonomous for decades, SSPA integrates a rigorous Future Sustainability Research layer via the non-speculative Sovereign Proof of Value ($SPOV) mechanism [3.2]. Managed by a secure token vesting contract with a 12-month cliff and 4-year linear release based on static allocation variables, SSPA rewards active humanitarian node validation, providing an unalterable, legal startup economic engine that places long-term civilizational human rights preservation above market speculation [3.2].
